# Verb Structure — Maitiro emashoko echiito

Shona verbs are agglutinative — prefixes and suffixes stack to encode subject, tense, object, and mood.

## Basic Structure

**Subject prefix + Tense marker + (Object prefix) + Verb root + Extension + Final vowel**

Example: **Ndi-no-mu-fund-is-a** = I (ndi) habitually (no) him/her (mu) teach (fund) cause (is) [final vowel] (a)
= "I teach him/her"

## Subject Prefixes

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st | ndi- | ti- |
| 2nd | u- | mu- |
| 3rd (cl.1) | a- | va- |

## Tense Markers

| Tense | Marker | Example (root: -tamba = play) |
|-------|--------|-------------------------------|
| Present habitual | -no- | Ndi-no-tamba (I play / I usually play) |
| Present progressive | -ri ku- | Ndi-ri ku-tamba (I am playing) |
| Past (recent) | -a- | Nda-tamba (I played) |
| Past (remote) | -aka- | Nda-ka-tamba (I played long ago) |
| Future | -cha- | Ndi-cha-tamba (I will play) |

## Negation

Add **ha-** before the subject prefix:

- Ha-ndi-tamb-i (I don't play) — note final vowel changes to -i
- Haa-tamba (He/she doesn't play)
- Ha-ti-tamb-i (We don't play)

## Imperative (Commands)

- **Tamba!** — Play! (singular)
- **Tambai!** — Play! (plural/respectful)

## Common Verb Extensions

| Extension | Suffix | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|---------|
| Applied | -ir-/-er- | Do for/at | tamb-ir-a (play for) |
| Causative | -is- | Cause to | tamb-is-a (make play) |
| Passive | -w- | Be done to | tamb-w-a (be played) |
| Reciprocal | -an- | Each other | tamb-an-a (play together) |
| Intensive | -is-is- | Do thoroughly | fund-is-is-a (teach thoroughly) |
