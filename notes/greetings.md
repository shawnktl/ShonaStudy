# Greetings — Kukwazisa

## Basic Greetings

- **Mhoro** — Hello (to one person)
- **Mhoroi** — Hello (to multiple people)
- **Makadii?** — How are you? (plural/respectful)
- **Wakadii?** — How are you? (singular/familiar)
- **Ndiripo** — I am well / I am here (response)
- **Tiripo** — We are well (response, plural)
- **Ndiripo kana makadiiwo** — I am well, and how are you?

## Time-of-Day Greetings

- **Mangwanani** — Good morning
- **Masikati** — Good afternoon
- **Manheru** — Good evening

Response pattern: *Mangwanani. Marara sei?* (Good morning. How did you sleep?)
Reply: *Ndarara kana mararawo* (I slept well if you slept well too)

## Introductions

- **Ndinonzi [name]** — I am called [name] / My name is [name]
- **Zita rangu ndi [name]** — My name is [name]
- **Munonzi ani?** — What is your name? (respectful)
- **Unonzi ani?** — What is your name? (familiar)
- **Ndinobva ku [place]** — I come from [place]

## Farewells

- **Chisarai zvakanaka** — Stay well (said by the one leaving)
- **Fambai zvakanaka** — Go well (said by the one staying)
- **Tinonana** — See you / We will see each other
- **Tinonana mangwana** — See you tomorrow

## Cultural Notes

- Greetings in Shona culture are not rushed — it's rude to skip to business
- The call-and-response pattern is fundamental — always reciprocate
- Use plural/respectful forms (ma-) with elders and strangers
- Clapping hands (kukombera) accompanies greetings as a sign of respect
